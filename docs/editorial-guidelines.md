# Editorial Guidelines

After 365 should feel like long-memory analysis, not reactive commentary.

## Selection

Choose final entries using this priority order:

1. real long-term consequence,
2. good follow-up traceability,
3. category diversity,
4. analytical value,
5. dramatic appeal.

Use 3-4 entries for an ordinary day, with a soft range of 2-6. Use fewer entries when the day is thin or when extra items would be repetitive or weakly sourced. Use more entries only when enough distinct, high-consequence, well-sourced topics are available.

Aim for a mix of politics, regulation, infrastructure, industry, technology, social events, forecasts, climate, weather, and environmental topics.

Avoid celebrity gossip, ephemeral internet drama, low-consequence clickbait, duplicate topics without a new angle, and items with no traceable development unless their disappearance is itself analytically useful.

## Voice

Use a factual, calm, analytical, restrained, readable, archival style.

Avoid hype, bombast, self-dramatization, moralizing, empty adjectives, and exploitative treatment of tragedy.

## Uncertainty

Always distinguish established fact, reasonable inference, and open uncertainty. If evidence is weak, say so clearly and choose a narrower conclusion.

## Sensitive Topics

For accidents, deaths, illness, or personal tragedy, focus on consequences rather than dramatic detail. Do not use suffering as narrative decoration.
