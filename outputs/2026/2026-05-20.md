## 1. Trump selects the Golden Dome missile-defense concept

**Original date:** 2025-05-20
**Type:** Defense / Space / Procurement
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-20, President Donald Trump announced that he had selected an architecture for the Golden Dome missile-defense program and named Space Force Gen. Michael Guetlein to lead it. AP and Reuters described the proposal as a multilayered homeland missile shield, including space-based elements, with Trump putting the initial cost at about $175 billion and saying it could be operational before the end of his term.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, Golden Dome has moved from political announcement to a major budget and feasibility dispute. AP reported on 2026-05-12 that a Congressional Budget Office analysis estimated a Golden Dome-like architecture could cost about $1.2 trillion over 20 years, far above the original White House figure. Reuters reported the same estimate and noted that CBO's illustrative architecture included a large space-based interceptor layer and warned that the system could be overwhelmed by a full-scale attack from Russia or China.

### Direct impacts
The announcement gave the program a name, leadership, and strategic direction, tying U.S. homeland missile defense to a more ambitious space-based architecture. It also created a new procurement track for missile defense, sensors, command-and-control systems, satellites, and possible orbital interceptors.

### Indirect impacts
The program became a focal point for broader questions about space weaponization, defense-industrial capacity, congressional oversight, and the gap between presidential cost claims and independent budget estimates. The one-year record suggests that Golden Dome's most immediate effect was not deployed capability but a durable fiscal and strategic debate.

### What original coverage missed
Initial coverage could identify the ambition and political symbolism of a national shield, but it could not measure the cost spread or the operational limits. The CBO follow-up showed that the program's cost and effectiveness depend heavily on still-undisclosed architecture choices.

### One-year assessment
The May 20 announcement was consequential because it put a space-heavy missile-defense project at the center of U.S. defense planning. One year later, its practical status remains uncertain: the concept is politically alive and funded in part, but the credible cost range and technical risks are much larger than the original presentation implied.

## 2. Hungary's parliament approves withdrawal from the International Criminal Court

**Original date:** 2025-05-20
**Type:** International Justice / Rule of Law
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-20, Hungary's parliament approved legislation to begin withdrawing from the International Criminal Court. Reuters reported that the bill passed 134 to 37 and followed Prime Minister Viktor Orban's accusation that the court had become political, shortly after Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu visited Hungary despite an ICC arrest warrant.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the withdrawal is not yet legally effective but has become a live test of Hungary's obligations. The Assembly of States Parties said Hungary deposited its withdrawal notification with the United Nations on 2025-06-02, making the withdrawal effective on 2026-06-02. ICC materials later stated that Hungary remained bound by Rome Statute duties until that date. Human Rights Watch reported in April 2026 that Prime Minister-elect Peter Magyar pledged to reverse or halt the move after Hungary's election.

### Direct impacts
The vote made Hungary the first European Union member state to approve leaving the Rome Statute system. It also converted a political dispute over the Netanyahu warrant into a formal legal process with a one-year withdrawal clock.

### Indirect impacts
The move weakened the appearance of EU consensus around the ICC at a time when the court faced pressure over Gaza, Ukraine, and U.S. sanctions against court officials. It also made Hungary's rule-of-law dispute with European institutions more directly connected to international criminal justice.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate coverage could register the parliamentary vote and its connection to Netanyahu's visit, but it could not know whether the withdrawal would survive Hungary's next political cycle. By the anniversary, the legal deadline was near, but a new political pledge to reverse course had complicated the outcome.

### One-year assessment
The vote was consequential because it exposed a break inside the EU over cooperation with the world's permanent criminal court. One year later, the legal process is still reversible only through political action, so the event's lasting significance depends on whether Hungary remains outside the Rome Statute after 2026-06-02 or re-enters the system.

## 3. Two senior aides to Mexico City's mayor are killed in a targeted attack

**Original date:** 2025-05-20
**Type:** Political Violence / Urban Security
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-20, gunmen killed Ximena Guzman, private secretary to Mexico City Mayor Clara Brugada, and Jose Munoz, a senior adviser, in the capital's Moderna neighborhood. AP and Reuters described the killings as a direct attack on two people close to one of Mexico's most powerful city officials.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the case has produced arrests but not full public clarity. AP reported in August 2025 that authorities arrested at least 13 people allegedly involved in what officials described as a carefully planned hit. On the anniversary, El Pais reported that the Mexico City security cabinet cited 18 detainees and an identified network, but still limited public detail on motive and command responsibility.

### Direct impacts
The attack killed two senior members of Brugada's inner team and forced Mexico City's government to respond to a political-security crisis in the middle of the capital. It also became a direct test of the city's investigative capacity and federal-local coordination.

### Indirect impacts
The killings challenged the perception that Mexico City was insulated from the kind of targeted political violence more often associated with other parts of the country. They also placed pressure on Brugada's security agenda early in her administration.

### What original coverage missed
Initial coverage could document the deaths and official condemnation, but not the scale of the alleged support network or the persistence of unanswered questions. The one-year record suggests a planned operation with arrests across logistical roles, while the motive and higher-level ordering remain less transparent.

### One-year assessment
The attack was consequential because it brought organized, targeted violence into the immediate orbit of Mexico City's top elected office. One year later, the case is not cold, but the distinction between arrests and full accountability remains important.

## 4. Google rolls out AI Mode in Search to U.S. users

**Original date:** 2025-05-20
**Type:** Technology / Search / Artificial Intelligence
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-20 at Google I/O, Google announced that AI Mode in Search would roll out to users in the United States. Google said the feature used a custom Gemini 2.5 model and a query fan-out technique to answer more complex, multi-part questions, moving beyond standard link results and AI Overviews.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, AI Mode has become part of Google's continuing redesign of Search rather than a one-off experiment. Google said in January 2026 that Search was using Gemini 3 for AI Overviews and that users could move from an AI Overview into an AI Mode conversation. Alphabet's Q1 2026 earnings release said AI experiences helped drive all-time-high Search query volume and 19 percent revenue growth in Search. At the same time, independent researchers and publishers continued to scrutinize source quality, traffic effects, and the possibility that AI summaries change the economics of the open web.

### Direct impacts
The rollout gave mainstream U.S. users a dedicated conversational search surface inside Google Search. It also made AI-generated synthesis and follow-up questions a first-party search behavior rather than a separate chatbot workflow.

### Indirect impacts
The change intensified pressure on publishers, search marketers, and regulators because answers could increasingly be consumed inside Google's interface without a traditional click. It also showed that Google was willing to alter the core search product while defending search-advertising growth.

### What original coverage missed
Launch coverage could identify the product shift, but it could not know whether the feature would remain peripheral or merge more deeply into Search. By the anniversary, Google had tied AI Mode to AI Overviews, upgraded the models, and reported continuing Search growth, while external research was beginning to measure reliability and publisher effects.

### One-year assessment
The May 20 rollout was consequential because it marked a public turn from search as ranked links toward search as an AI-mediated answer and task interface. One year later, the business appears intact, but the information-ecosystem costs and reliability questions remain unsettled.
