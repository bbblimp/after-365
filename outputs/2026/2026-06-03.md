## 1. Lee Jae-myung wins South Korea's snap presidential election

**Original date:** 2025-06-03
**Type:** Elections / Democratic Recovery / Executive Power
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-03, South Koreans voted in a snap presidential election called after Yoon Suk Yeol's failed martial-law declaration, impeachment, and removal from office. Liberal Democratic Party candidate Lee Jae-myung won with 49.42 percent of the vote against conservative Kim Moon-soo's 41.15 percent, with turnout reported as the highest presidential-election turnout since 1997.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the election looks like the institutional handoff that ended the acute phase of the martial-law crisis. Lee took office immediately on 2025-06-04, formed a government with a Democratic Party legislative majority, and by spring 2026 was polling in the mid-to-high 60s in Gallup Korea surveys, aided by public approval of economic and diplomatic management. The deeper political division exposed by the crisis remained, but elected institutions continued to function.

### Direct impacts
The vote replaced an impeached conservative administration with a liberal presidency, gave Lee direct control of the executive for a single five-year term, and allowed South Korea to move from interim crisis management back to ordinary presidential government.

### Indirect impacts
The result changed the tone of South Korean foreign and economic policy, with Lee presenting himself as pragmatic while inheriting trade pressure, demographic strain, and the need to repair confidence after martial law. It also left the conservative People Power Party weakened by association with Yoon's failed emergency rule.

### What original coverage missed
Election-night coverage could identify the verdict against the martial-law episode, but it could not yet show whether Lee would stabilize politics or simply preside over another polarized cycle. The first year suggests partial stabilization rather than full reconciliation.

### One-year assessment
The June 3 election was consequential because it converted a constitutional rupture into an electoral succession. One year later, the outcome appears to have restored executive legitimacy and public confidence without erasing South Korea's partisan and institutional strains.

## 2. The Dutch government collapses after Geert Wilders quits the coalition

**Original date:** 2025-06-03
**Type:** Coalition Politics / Migration Policy / European Democracy
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-03, the Dutch government collapsed when Geert Wilders withdrew his Party for Freedom from Prime Minister Dick Schoof's four-party right-wing coalition. Wilders said coalition partners would not accept his demands for much tougher asylum measures; Schoof called the collapse unnecessary and irresponsible and moved to resign the cabinet.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the collapse had led to an October 2025 snap election and a different governing direction. D66 narrowly emerged ahead of Wilders's PVV in votes while the PVV lost support compared with its 2023 result. On 2026-02-23, the Netherlands swore in Rob Jetten's minority cabinet of D66, VVD, and CDA, making the June collapse a failed attempt by Wilders to convert asylum leverage into more durable governing power.

### Direct impacts
The decision ended the Schoof cabinet after less than a year, placed the Netherlands under caretaker government, and forced an early election instead of allowing the coalition to continue toward its 2028 schedule.

### Indirect impacts
The episode showed both the influence and the limits of far-right participation in Dutch coalition politics. It also prolonged policy uncertainty on migration, housing, nitrogen, defense, and public finance while parties waited for a new parliamentary mandate and then negotiated a cabinet.

### What original coverage missed
Initial coverage rightly focused on asylum policy and Wilders's exit, but it could not yet show the electoral cost. The later outcome suggested that collapsing the cabinet did not strengthen the PVV's governing position.

### One-year assessment
The June 3 collapse was consequential because it turned a migration-policy standoff into a national election and reset Dutch coalition arithmetic. One year later, it stands as a case in which disruptive leverage produced instability but not a clearer mandate for the party that triggered it.

## 3. A UN aid convoy is attacked near Al Koma in North Darfur

**Original date:** 2025-06-03
**Type:** Humanitarian Access / Civilian Protection / Armed Conflict
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-03, WFP and UNICEF said a joint humanitarian convoy had been attacked near Al Koma in North Darfur the previous night. Five convoy members were killed and several others were injured as the 15-truck convoy tried to carry food and nutrition supplies toward besieged El Fasher.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the attack reads as an early warning of how dangerous and constrained aid delivery to Darfur would remain. WFP later condemned renewed attacks on its trucks and facilities, and UN agencies warned in May 2026 that nearly 19.5 million people in Sudan faced crisis levels of acute food insecurity, with parts of Darfur and Kordofan at risk of famine and hundreds of thousands of children at risk of severe malnutrition.

### Direct impacts
The attack killed humanitarian workers, damaged supplies, interrupted a rare convoy toward El Fasher, and forced agencies to confront the immediate security risk of cross-line aid delivery.

### Indirect impacts
The attack reinforced the broader pattern in Sudan's war: civilians were not only threatened by direct violence, but also by the obstruction, destruction, and underfunding of relief systems. It made access, not only food availability, central to the famine-risk assessment.

### What original coverage missed
Initial reports could identify the convoy's purpose and casualties, but they could not yet show the persistence of access failure. The one-year record shows that the problem was structural, not a single-route breakdown.

### One-year assessment
The Al Koma convoy attack was consequential because it linked battlefield violence directly to the mechanics of famine prevention. One year later, Sudan's hunger emergency remained severe, and the attack stands as a marker of how aid work itself became part of the war's hazard landscape.

## 4. Human Rights Watch reports M23 executions of civilians in Goma

**Original date:** 2025-06-03
**Type:** War Crimes Evidence / Armed Conflict / Accountability
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
On 2025-06-03, Human Rights Watch reported that Rwanda-backed M23 fighters had summarily executed at least 21 civilians, and probably more, in Goma on 2025-02-22 and 2025-02-23. The report said the killings occurred after M23's capture of the city and described them as war crimes; M23 denied the allegations in Reuters-linked coverage.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the report fits into a larger accountability record rather than a resolved case. The DRC and Rwanda signed a U.S.-mediated peace agreement later in June 2025, but Human Rights Watch's 2026 country review said the human-rights and humanitarian situation worsened significantly in 2025 amid abuses in the east. Later reporting also described continued M23 detention practices and attacks affecting civilians and aid workers in Goma.

### Direct impacts
The report documented alleged executions, named the chain of concern around Rwanda-backed M23 control of Goma, and supplied evidence for governments and mediators that were already pursuing diplomatic pressure.

### Indirect impacts
The findings complicated peace diplomacy by underscoring that ceasefire and mineral-security talks could not be separated from accountability for civilian killings, detention abuses, and possible war crimes.

### What original coverage missed
Initial reporting on Goma's fall focused on military control and regional escalation. The HRW report showed how control of the city also produced a civilian-protection and evidence-preservation problem that would remain relevant after diplomatic agreements were signed.

### One-year assessment
The June 3 report was consequential because it made alleged M23 abuses in Goma harder to treat as rumor or battlefield noise. One year later, it remains part of the evidentiary record against which peace implementation and regional diplomacy should be judged.
