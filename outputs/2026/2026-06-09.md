## 1. Kenyan police tear gas protesters after Albert Ojwang dies in custody

**Original date:** 2025-06-09
**Type:** Police Accountability / Civil Liberties / Protest
**Confidence:** High

### Then

Police initially attributed his fatal head injuries to hitting his head against a cell wall, while activists questioned that account and linked the case to wider complaints about police abuse.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the case had become a continuing police-accountability matter rather than a short-lived protest. ### Direct impacts
The immediate impact was public protest in Nairobi and pressure on police authorities to explain how a social-media-related arrest ended in death inside a central police station.

### Indirect impacts
The case renewed scrutiny of Kenyan policing after the 2024 finance-bill protest crackdown. It also turned a defamation-style complaint by a senior police official into a broader test of custody safeguards, police oversight, and public trust.

### What original coverage missed

### One-year assessment
The June 9 protest was consequential because it helped prevent Ojwang's death from being treated as an isolated custody incident. One year later, the outcome remained unresolved, but the public and legal pressure had made the case a durable accountability marker.

**Type:** Trade Policy / Export Controls / Critical Minerals
**Confidence:** High

### Then

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the London talks look like an early stage in a longer export-control bargain. The direct effect was to keep negotiations alive and temporarily reduce the risk that the Geneva tariff truce would collapse over critical minerals and technology restrictions.

### Indirect impacts
The talks clarified that U.S.-China economic conflict had moved beyond headline tariff rates. Rare earths, semiconductor tools, aircraft equipment, student visas, maritime fees, and anti-transshipment enforcement all became part of the same strategic bargaining space.

### What original coverage missed
Opening-day coverage could identify market sensitivity and the negotiators' agenda, but it could not yet show the two-day framework, the later October 2025 one-year truce, or the persistence of higher effective tariffs into 2026.

### One-year assessment
The London talks were not a settlement. Their importance was procedural and strategic: they helped keep a channel open while demonstrating that export controls and critical minerals had become central instruments of economic statecraft.

## 3. Philippine Senate passes the Anti-POGO Act on final reading

**Original date:** 2025-06-09
**Type:** Online Gambling / Organized Crime / Legislation
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-09, the Philippine Senate approved Senate Bill 2868, the Anti-POGO Act of 2025, on third and final reading. The measure sought to institutionalize the government's ban on Philippine Offshore Gaming Operators after official concern over crime, human trafficking, torture, and public-order risks associated with some POGO hubs.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the Senate vote had become part of enacted law. President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. signed Republic Act 12312 on 2025-10-23, and the Presidential Communications Office said the law repealed the earlier statute that taxed offshore gaming operations. The law declares offshore gaming and related operations illegal and sets prison terms, fines, deportation after sentence for foreign offenders, and a permanent re-entry ban.

### Direct impacts
The Senate vote moved the proposal from executive policy and campaign promise toward a statutory ban that could bind future administrations.

### Indirect impacts
The measure marked a reversal from a regulated-and-taxed offshore gaming model to a criminal-prohibition model. It also linked online gambling regulation to wider concerns about transnational crime, immigration control, investment-zone integrity, and corruption.

### What original coverage missed
Coverage of the Senate vote could not yet know whether the bill would survive the remaining legislative steps or what penalties and implementation framing would appear in the final law.

### One-year assessment
The June 9 vote mattered because it was not merely symbolic. It helped produce a completed legal ban that changed the status of an industry the Philippine government had previously regulated and taxed.
