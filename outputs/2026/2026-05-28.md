## 1. A U.S. trade court blocks most Trump emergency tariffs

**Original date:** 2025-05-28
**Type:** Trade Law / Executive Power / Global Markets
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-28, the U.S. Court of International Trade blocked most of President Donald Trump's sweeping tariffs imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act. The three-judge panel held that the statute did not give the president open-ended authority to impose broad import duties tied to trade deficits and related emergencies.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the May ruling looks like the opening move in a larger judicial limit on emergency tariff power rather than a temporary market event. The tariffs were quickly stayed while appeals proceeded, but in February 2026 the Supreme Court rejected the use of IEEPA for those global tariffs. The administration then turned to other trade authorities, including a temporary 10 percent global tariff that the trade court also ruled against in May 2026, though with a narrower immediate remedy.

### Direct impacts
The immediate effect was legal uncertainty around the administration's central tariff architecture. Importers, trading partners, and markets had to price not only tariff rates but also whether the legal basis for the rates would survive appellate review.

### Indirect impacts
The decision shifted the tariff fight from ordinary trade policy into separation-of-powers doctrine. It also encouraged the administration to search for alternative statutory tools, showing that judicial limits on one emergency power did not end the broader protectionist strategy.

### What original coverage missed
Same-day coverage could see the size of the legal setback, but it could not yet show whether appellate courts would preserve, narrow, or erase the ruling. The one-year record shows that the May 28 decision became a foundation for a broader Supreme Court constraint on IEEPA tariffs.

### One-year assessment
The ruling was consequential because it challenged the premise that a president could use emergency economic powers as a general tariff code. One year later, its significance lies less in the brief market reaction than in the durable constitutional and statutory boundary it helped establish.

## 2. Flooding devastates Mokwa, Nigeria

**Original date:** 2025-05-28
**Type:** Disaster / Climate Risk / Infrastructure
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On the night of 2025-05-28 into the early hours of 2025-05-29, torrential rain triggered severe flooding in Mokwa, a market and transport town in Niger State, Nigeria. Initial reports grew quickly from dozens of deaths to more than 100, with officials warning that the toll would rise as search operations continued.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the Mokwa flood stands as a local disaster with national warning value. AP later reported the death toll at at least 200 and said rescue efforts had been halted because officials no longer expected survivors. WHO and UN follow-up described thousands displaced, disrupted health services, and a response focused on trauma care, disease surveillance, food relief, and restoration of basic health access.

### Direct impacts
The flood killed at least 200 people, displaced thousands, damaged homes and community facilities, and interrupted health services in and around Mokwa. It also produced immediate public-health risks from contaminated water, trauma injuries, and bodies trapped under debris.

### Indirect impacts
The disaster exposed the danger of dense settlement patterns, weak drainage, and inadequate flood-control infrastructure in a town that serves as a trading link between northern farmers and southern markets. It also reinforced that Nigeria's recurring flood season is both a climate-risk problem and a local infrastructure problem.

### What original coverage missed
Early coverage necessarily treated the toll as provisional. Later reporting showed the disaster's scale more clearly and documented the health-system and displacement consequences that were not visible while rescue work was still under way.

### One-year assessment
Mokwa mattered because it was not only a sudden weather tragedy; it was a demonstration of how intense rainfall becomes mass casualty when drainage, land use, emergency response, and health systems are weak. One year later, the event remains a useful marker for judging whether flood-risk warnings are turning into local protection.

## 3. A fatal Thailand-Cambodia border clash reopens an old dispute

**Original date:** 2025-05-28
**Type:** Border Conflict / Southeast Asian Politics / Diplomacy
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-28, Thai and Cambodian soldiers briefly exchanged fire in a disputed border area, killing one Cambodian soldier. Both sides gave conflicting accounts of who entered the contested area and who fired first, while local commanders ended the clash after roughly 10 minutes.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the skirmish looks like the trigger for a much larger bilateral crisis. AP reported in June 2025 that the confrontation helped drive border restrictions, economic boycotts, and domestic pressure on Thailand's prime minister. Reuters later described the May killing as the point from which the two countries were on a conflict footing before deadly July fighting displaced more than 300,000 people and required a ceasefire.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was one soldier's death and a military de-escalation effort by army chiefs the following day. The clash also put disputed border management back into national political debate in both countries.

### Indirect impacts
The incident fed nationalist pressure, border closures, troop reinforcement, and a diplomatic crisis that weakened Thailand's already fragile coalition politics. It also showed how unresolved demarcation disputes can move from symbolic contestation to armed escalation with little warning.

### What original coverage missed
Initial reporting could reasonably describe the event as a brief clash. Later events showed that the death carried more political weight than the duration of the firefight suggested.

### One-year assessment
The May 28 clash was consequential because it converted a dormant or managed border dispute into an active crisis. One year later, its importance is as an ignition point: the casualty did not by itself determine later fighting, but it created the political and military conditions in which escalation became easier.

## 4. WMO says 2025-2029 temperatures are likely to stay near record levels

**Original date:** 2025-05-28
**Type:** Climate Science / Risk Forecasting / Global Governance
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-28, the World Meteorological Organization released its Global Annual to Decadal Climate Update for 2025-2029. It forecast an 80 percent chance that at least one year in the period would exceed 2024 as the warmest year on record, and a 70 percent chance that the five-year average for 2025-2029 would exceed 1.5 degrees C above the 1850-1900 average.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the first year of the forecast period did not exceed 2024, but it confirmed the broader high-temperature baseline. WMO's 2026 State of the Global Climate report said 2025 was the second or third hottest year on record at about 1.43 degrees C above the 1850-1900 average, and that 2015-2025 were the hottest 11 years on record. Copernicus separately ranked 2025 as the third hottest year on record.

### Direct impacts
The update gave governments, insurers, infrastructure planners, and climate-risk agencies a near-term probability frame rather than a distant end-century scenario. Its figures helped translate the Paris Agreement's 1.5 degrees C threshold into operational risk language for the remainder of the decade.

### Indirect impacts
The report reinforced that short-term variability, including La Nina conditions, does not erase the long-term warming trend. It also made the next several annual temperature reports politically and scientifically important tests of whether temporary 1.5 degrees C exceedances are becoming more persistent.

### What original coverage missed
Original coverage could state the probabilities, but it could not yet show how the first forecast year would rank. The 2025 outcome did not fulfill the warmest-year probability immediately, yet it supported the report's central claim that global temperatures were staying near record levels.

### One-year assessment
The WMO update was consequential because it narrowed climate risk from abstract future warming to a five-year planning horizon. One year later, the forecast's value is not that every high-end outcome happened at once, but that even a cooler La Nina-influenced year remained among the hottest measured.
