## 1. Israel strikes Iranian nuclear and military sites, starting direct Iran-Israel war

**Original date:** 2025-06-13
**Type:** Regional Conflict / Nuclear Security / Middle East
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
On 2025-06-13, Israel launched major strikes on Iranian nuclear, military, missile and leadership targets, including sites linked to the Natanz nuclear complex and senior commanders. Iran retaliated with drones and missiles, turning a long shadow conflict into open direct warfare.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the event is understood as the opening of the Twelve-Day War and as the point from which the confrontation broadened into U.S. strikes, Iranian retaliation against U.S. and Gulf targets, disrupted shipping and repeated ceasefire diplomacy. A fragile ceasefire had not erased the risk: in early June 2026, Iran again launched missiles at Israel, while mediators and the United States were still trying to turn temporary pauses into a durable settlement.

### Direct impacts
The strikes killed Iranian military leaders and nuclear scientists, damaged nuclear and military infrastructure, triggered Iranian missile and drone barrages, closed or disrupted regional airspace, and forced governments and markets to price in a wider Middle East war.

### Indirect impacts
The war moved the Iran nuclear question from inspections and negotiation into a military-diplomatic crisis. It increased pressure on the IAEA safeguards system, complicated U.S.-Iran talks, disrupted the Strait of Hormuz risk calculus, and made Lebanon, Gulf bases and Israeli home-front security part of the same escalation chain.

### What original coverage missed
First-day coverage could identify the scale of the Israeli attack and immediate retaliation, but it could not yet know the duration of the war, the extent of later U.S. involvement, the ceasefire structure, or whether the strikes would delay Iran's nuclear capacity without hardening its strategic incentives.

### One-year assessment
This was the clearest major-event exception on the date. One year later, its significance is not a resolved military outcome but the creation of a more volatile regional order: nuclear diplomacy continued, but under conditions shaped by destroyed infrastructure, direct missile exchanges and repeated attempts to prevent a return to open war.

## 2. Trump approves Nippon Steel's U.S. Steel takeover under a national-security agreement

**Original date:** 2025-06-13
**Type:** Foreign Investment / Industrial Policy / Corporate Governance
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-13, the Trump administration approved Nippon Steel's acquisition of U.S. Steel after a revived national-security review and new conditions. The arrangement transformed a blocked foreign takeover into an approved transaction with investment promises and a U.S. government golden share over sensitive corporate decisions.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the takeover had closed and U.S. Steel was operating as part of Nippon Steel under a national-security agreement. The durable feature was not only Japanese ownership of a famous U.S. steelmaker, but the precedent of approving foreign investment through direct federal governance rights over headquarters, board, production and investment commitments.

### Direct impacts
The approval cleared the path for a roughly $14 billion transaction, kept U.S. Steel's headquarters in Pittsburgh, attached billions in promised U.S. investment, and gave the U.S. government special approval rights over major company actions.

### Indirect impacts
The deal became a test case for how national security, labor politics, tariffs and allied-country investment can be blended into corporate governance. It may encourage future buyers of sensitive U.S. assets to offer explicit domestic-investment and government-oversight concessions.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate coverage focused on whether the deal would survive. The later importance lay in the details: the golden-share powers, the shift of control after Trump leaves office, and the use of industrial-policy conditions as the price of approval.

### One-year assessment
The U.S. Steel decision was consequential less because it saved or sold a single company than because it showed a new model of conditional openness. The United States accepted allied foreign ownership while reserving unusually direct state influence over a private industrial asset.

## 3. China and Hong Kong authorities launch first publicly known joint national-security operation

**Original date:** 2025-06-13
**Type:** Civil Liberties / National Security Law / Hong Kong
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-13, AP reported that Beijing's national-security authorities in Hong Kong and the Hong Kong police had launched their first publicly known joint operation under the 2020 national security law, raiding homes of six people suspected of colluding with foreign forces and seizing documents and devices.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the operation stands as one piece of a broader enforcement consolidation around the fifth anniversary of the law. Hong Kong's last active pro-democracy party later disbanded, national-security cases continued, and civil-society space remained sharply constrained.

### Direct impacts
Six people's homes and an organization's office were searched, materials were seized, and the people involved were required to surrender travel documents while the authorities investigated suspected collusion with foreign forces.

### Indirect impacts
The operation publicly normalized direct cooperation between Beijing's Hong Kong national-security office and local police. It signaled that the boundary between mainland and local enforcement was becoming more operational rather than only constitutional or rhetorical.

### What original coverage missed
The first reports could not identify the suspects or organization, nor could they show how the episode would fit into the fifth-anniversary climate of prosecutions, dissolutions and self-censorship. Later coverage made clearer that enforcement pressure was affecting not only defendants but organized political life.

### One-year assessment
The operation was not the largest Hong Kong national-security case, but it was institutionally important. It marked a visible step from parallel authority toward joint enforcement, and one year later it reads as part of the steady narrowing of Hong Kong's permitted political space.
