## 1. Nicusor Dan wins Romania's rerun presidential election

**Original date:** 2025-05-18
**Type:** Politics / Elections / European Institutions
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-18, Bucharest mayor Nicusor Dan won Romania's rerun presidential runoff against hard-right AUR leader George Simion. The vote followed the annulment of the previous presidential election and was widely framed as a choice over Romania's pro-European direction.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, Dan's victory looks less like a clean institutional reset than a temporary easing of a deeper political crisis. Reuters reported on 2026-05-05 that lawmakers toppled Prime Minister Ilie Bolojan's pro-EU government in a no-confidence vote, putting pressure on sovereign ratings, EU funds access, and the leu. Dan, as president, was left to manage another round of coalition negotiations while AUR led opinion polls.

### Direct impacts
The immediate impact was to block Simion's bid for the presidency, close the rerun election process, and keep the head of state aligned with Romania's EU and NATO orientation. Dan was sworn in later in May 2025, ending the most acute phase of the annulled-election crisis.

### Indirect impacts
The vote did not remove Romania's fiscal and coalition problems. It instead shifted responsibility to a president whose formal powers include nominating a prime minister, while the same anti-establishment and hard-right pressures remained strong in parliament and polling.

### What original coverage missed
Original coverage could capture the geopolitical stakes of the runoff, but it could not show whether the new presidency would stabilize governing coalitions. One year later, the answer is mixed: the presidency held its pro-European line, but the governing arrangement remained fragile.

### One-year assessment
Dan's May 18 win was consequential because it prevented an immediate hard-right presidential turn after an annulled election. Its limit is equally important: Romania's institutional crisis was contained, not solved, and by the first anniversary the country was again negotiating government formation under fiscal and political pressure.

## 2. A suicide bomber attacks recruits at a Mogadishu military camp

**Original date:** 2025-05-18
**Type:** Security / Insurgency / State Capacity
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-18, a suicide bomber targeted young recruits waiting to register at a military camp in Mogadishu. AP reported at least 13 dead and 21 wounded, including civilian passers-by, with witnesses saying the attacker had disguised himself as a recruit.

### State after 365 days
One year later, the attack reads as part of a persistent state-capacity problem rather than an isolated bombing. The BTI 2026 Somalia country report described al-Shabaab as Somalia's most serious security threat and said it continued regular attacks on government, military, checkpoint, and civilian targets while maintaining shadow governance even in areas formally under government or allied control.

### Direct impacts
The bombing killed recruits and civilians, wounded others, disrupted military enlistment, and demonstrated the vulnerability of recruitment sites that gather large numbers of lightly protected young people.

### Indirect impacts
The attack reinforced the difficulty of expanding Somali security forces while al-Shabaab retains the ability to penetrate or surveil state targets. It also underlined the security burden facing Somalia and its African Union partners during the transition from older international missions to newer security arrangements.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate reporting could establish the casualties and the apparent method, but it could not yet situate the attack in a full-year pattern of insurgent pressure. The later country assessment shows that the core problem remained durable: al-Shabaab's operational reach and Somalia's limited ability to secure state institutions.

### One-year assessment
The Mogadishu bombing mattered because it struck the state at the point where it was trying to replenish military manpower. After 365 days, it stands as a concise example of why recruitment, training, and territorial control remain linked challenges in Somalia's fight against al-Shabaab.

## 3. India's PSLV-C61 mission fails to place EOS-09 in orbit

**Original date:** 2025-05-18
**Type:** Science / Space / Earth Observation
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
On 2025-05-18, India's PSLV-C61 launch of the EOS-09 Earth-observation satellite failed after a technical issue in the launch vehicle's third stage. ISRO chair V. Narayanan said there was a fall in chamber pressure in the motor case and that the mission could not be accomplished.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the failure remains notable because it involved the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle, one of India's established launch systems, and the lost payload was meant for operational Earth-observation use. ISRO's 2025-26 annual report still listed the PSLV-C61/EOS-09 launch as an attempted mission and described EOS-N1 as the follow-on spacecraft planned for PSLV-C62. Reporting on the January 2026 PSLV-C62 flight described that mission as the first PSLV liftoff since the May 2025 failure and said it also appeared to encounter an anomaly.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was the loss of the EOS-09 mission objective and the absence of the planned radar-imaging satellite from its intended orbit. The failure also interrupted the usual assumption of routine PSLV reliability.

### Indirect impacts
The event increased scrutiny of quality control, failure analysis, and return-to-flight discipline within India's launch program. Because Earth-observation satellites support civilian, environmental, and security-related applications, the lost payload had consequences beyond a single launch manifest item.

### What original coverage missed
Original coverage could identify the broad third-stage pressure issue, but it could not yet assess the full technical cause or whether the next PSLV flight would restore confidence. One year later, public information still supports caution rather than a definitive technical conclusion.

### One-year assessment
The PSLV-C61 failure was consequential as a reliability warning inside an otherwise ambitious Indian space program. It did not derail India's space agenda, but it turned a routine Earth-observation launch into a case that future PSLV missions would be judged against.

## 4. Joe Biden's office discloses an aggressive prostate-cancer diagnosis

**Original date:** 2025-05-18
**Type:** Health / Public Life / U.S. Politics
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-18, Joe Biden's office disclosed that the former U.S. president had been diagnosed with an aggressive prostate cancer that had spread to the bone. The office said the cancer appeared hormone-sensitive, allowing for effective management, while Biden and his family reviewed treatment options.

### State after 365 days
One year later, the story is principally a health and public-life record rather than a policy event. AP reported in October 2025 that Biden underwent radiation and hormone treatment, completed a round of radiation therapy, and then made public remarks later that month. No public evidence in the year after the disclosure showed a direct governing consequence, because Biden was already out of office.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was a major medical disclosure by a former president only months after he left office. It clarified that Biden had metastatic disease and moved public reporting from speculation about general age and fitness toward a specific diagnosis and treatment course.

### Indirect impacts
The disclosure fed broader debates about presidential health transparency, aging political leadership, and how much the public should know about senior officials' medical conditions. Those debates were politically charged, but the documented health updates themselves were limited and treatment-focused.

### What original coverage missed
Initial coverage could not know which treatment path Biden would follow or whether the diagnosis would sharply curtail his public activity. Follow-up reporting showed radiation and hormone therapy, completion of a radiation round, and at least some later public appearances.

### One-year assessment
The diagnosis was consequential as a public record about a recently departed president's health, but its one-year impact was bounded. It became part of the archive of Biden's post-presidency and the continuing argument over health disclosure in U.S. politics, not a direct driver of government policy.
