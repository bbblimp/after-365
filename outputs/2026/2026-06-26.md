## 1. Supreme Court lets states exclude Planned Parenthood from Medicaid programs

**Original date:** 2025-06-26
**Type:** Health Law / Medicaid / Reproductive Health
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-26, the U.S. Supreme Court decided Medina v. Planned Parenthood South Atlantic in a 6-3 ruling. The Court held that Medicaid's provider-choice language did not create an individual right enforceable through patient lawsuits under the federal civil-rights statute, allowing South Carolina to exclude Planned Parenthood from its Medicaid program.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the decision stands as part of a broader funding and access fight rather than an isolated South Carolina dispute. Later federal legislation and litigation over Medicaid payments to Planned Parenthood affiliates kept the issue active, and the ruling remains important because it limited one of the main private enforcement paths Medicaid beneficiaries had used to challenge provider exclusions.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was to lift the legal barrier that had blocked South Carolina's exclusion of Planned Parenthood from Medicaid reimbursement for non-abortion services. The decision also signaled to other conservative-led states that similar provider-exclusion efforts were less vulnerable to patient suits based on the Medicaid Act's any-qualified-provider provision.

### Indirect impacts
The indirect effect was procedural as much as substantive. By narrowing private enforcement under a spending-clause program, the ruling raised questions about how Medicaid beneficiaries can enforce federal conditions when state agencies restrict access, and it pushed more of the dispute toward administrative enforcement, state policy choices, and separate constitutional or statutory challenges.

### What original coverage missed
Same-day coverage could identify the immediate reproductive-health stakes, but it could not yet show how quickly the ruling would interact with later congressional defunding efforts or how much litigation would shift from provider-choice claims to other theories.

### One-year assessment
The decision was consequential because it changed the litigation terrain around Medicaid access. One year later, its practical effect is clearest in the combination of state-level provider exclusions and federal defunding battles: the Court did not decide abortion policy directly, but it made it harder for Medicaid patients to use federal court to preserve access to a disfavored provider.

## 2. Nigeria's president signs a major tax overhaul into law

**Original date:** 2025-06-26
**Type:** Tax Reform / Fiscal Governance / West Africa
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
On 2025-06-26, Nigerian President Bola Tinubu signed a package of tax reform laws. Contemporary reporting and subsequent professional summaries described the package as a broad overhaul intended to simplify tax administration, reduce the company income tax rate from 30 percent to 25 percent, exempt very small businesses from company tax, and reorganize federal revenue collection.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the reforms are no longer only a presidential signing ceremony. They have become an implementation project: tax administrators, companies, state governments, and advisers have had to interpret new rules, prepare for phased effective dates, and work through drafting and administrative issues. The appointment of tax-reform chair Taiwo Oyedele to senior finance posts in 2026 also showed that the reform agenda remained central to the government's economic program.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was to create a new statutory framework for revenue administration and business taxation. The package aimed to broaden Nigeria's tax base, lower formal rates for companies, exempt smaller enterprises, and strengthen the federal tax authority's administrative role.

### Indirect impacts
The indirect impact was to shift Nigeria's fiscal debate from whether a major tax rewrite would pass to whether the state could implement it without imposing new uncertainty on taxpayers. Professional advisers and opposition figures later focused on inconsistencies, transition rules, and readiness, showing that implementation capacity became part of the reform's significance.

### What original coverage missed
Signing-day coverage could capture the political victory and headline rates, but it could not yet test whether the reforms would improve compliance, raise the tax-to-GDP ratio, or simplify administration in practice.

### One-year assessment
The overhaul was consequential because it attempted to remake Nigeria's revenue system at a time of fiscal pressure. One year later, its importance lies less in any single rate change than in the implementation burden: the laws gave Nigeria a new framework, but the outcome depends on administrative capacity, taxpayer trust, and whether simplification survives the transition from statute to practice.

## 3. Lalo Schifrin dies after a career that reshaped screen music

**Original date:** 2025-06-26
**Type:** Film Music / Cultural Legacy / Popular Media
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-26, Argentine-born composer, pianist, arranger, and conductor Lalo Schifrin died in Los Angeles at 93. Obituary coverage emphasized his jazz-rooted film and television scores, especially the Mission: Impossible theme, along with work for Bullitt, Cool Hand Luke, Dirty Harry, Enter the Dragon, and other screen projects.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, Schifrin's death has settled into the archival record as the close of a long career rather than the beginning of a new controversy or institution. His work remains present through the continuing Mission: Impossible franchise, television reuse, sampling, soundtrack reissues, and the wider language of action and espionage scoring that he helped define.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was commemorative: major outlets reassessed a composer whose public identity was often reduced to one famous theme but whose catalog spanned jazz, orchestral, television, and film music. The death also prompted renewed attention to the Latin American and jazz foundations of several familiar Hollywood sounds.

### Indirect impacts
The broader significance is that Schifrin's work shows how a television theme and a set of genre scores can outlive their original productions. His rhythms, orchestration, and strategic use of silence helped shape the sound of late-20th-century screen tension and remained available to later composers, producers, and sampling artists.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate obituaries could summarize the career, but they could not fully separate durable influence from memorial praise. A year later, the evidence still points to continuity rather than reinvention: the work persists because it was already embedded in recurring franchises and popular memory.

### One-year assessment
Schifrin's death was not a policy event, but it was culturally consequential. One year later, the clearest assessment is restrained: his passing did not alter the entertainment industry, but it marked the end of a career whose musical vocabulary had already become part of how audiences recognize suspense, pursuit, and espionage on screen.
