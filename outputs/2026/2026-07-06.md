## 1. The Dalai Lama turns 90 after setting a succession boundary

**Original date:** 2025-07-06
**Type:** Religion / Succession / China-India-Tibet Politics
**Confidence:** High

### Then
The 14th Dalai Lama marked his 90th birthday in Dharamshala, India, surrounded by Tibetan Buddhist followers, monks, nuns, diaspora figures, and international supporters. The celebration followed his statement that the Dalai Lama institution would continue and that the Gaden Phodrang Trust would have sole responsibility for recognizing his future reincarnation.

### State after 365 days
One year later, the birthday reads less as a ceremonial milestone than as a succession marker. The Dalai Lama remained alive at 91, but the central issue had already been clarified: the exile institution had asserted exclusive religious authority, while China continued to claim that any future Dalai Lama must be approved under Chinese procedures.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was to reduce ambiguity inside the Tibetan exile community about whether the Dalai Lama lineage would end. It also gave governments, monasteries, and diaspora institutions a clearer position around which to organize before a future succession crisis.

### Indirect impacts
The statement sharpened a likely future dispute between a Tibetan-recognized successor and any Beijing-backed claimant. It also kept India in a sensitive position because the Tibetan government-in-exile and the Dalai Lama's residence are on Indian soil while New Delhi manages a difficult relationship with Beijing.

### What original coverage missed
Birthday coverage could show the public reverence and the immediate symbolism, but it could not yet show whether the succession statement would endure as the main institutional fact of the year. By the 91st birthday, the succession boundary remained the durable consequence.

### One-year assessment
The event was consequential because it converted a long-anticipated birthday into a concrete institutional position. It did not resolve the succession conflict, but it made the lines of authority clearer before the conflict becomes unavoidable.

## 2. The expanded BRICS summit opens in Rio with governance and climate ambitions

**Original date:** 2025-07-06
**Type:** Multilateral Diplomacy / Global Governance / Climate Finance
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
Leaders and senior representatives from the expanded BRICS grouping opened a two-day summit in Rio de Janeiro under Brazil's chairship. The meeting emphasized Global South cooperation, reform of global governance, climate finance, trade tensions, artificial intelligence, and the practical difficulty of finding common positions among a much larger bloc.

### State after 365 days
One year later, the Rio summit looks like a handoff point rather than a decisive breakthrough. India had taken the 2026 BRICS chairship and built its official theme around resilience, innovation, cooperation, and sustainability, preserving several Rio priorities while also reframing them through New Delhi's own diplomatic agenda.

### Direct impacts
The direct output was a summit declaration and a renewed claim that BRICS could speak for developing countries on climate finance, institutional reform, trade protectionism, and AI governance. The meeting also demonstrated the scale of the enlarged grouping, with Indonesia newly inside BRICS and partner countries attached to the wider process.

### Indirect impacts
The summit exposed the limits of expansion. The absence of Xi Jinping in person and Vladimir Putin's remote participation reduced the meeting's political force, while divisions among China, India, Russia, Gulf states, Iran, Brazil, and African members constrained how far the bloc could go on currency, security, and conflict language.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate coverage could describe the tension between ambition and disunity, but it could not yet show whether the agenda would survive the rotation to India. The 2026 chairship suggests continuity in broad themes, not evidence of a unified geopolitical bloc.

### One-year assessment
The Rio summit mattered as an institutional waypoint for an enlarged BRICS. Its consequence was not a single binding decision, but the consolidation of a forum that can keep climate finance, AI governance, and Global South representation on the agenda while still struggling to act cohesively.

## 3. Typhoon Danas makes an unusual western Taiwan landfall

**Original date:** 2025-07-06
**Type:** Disaster / Tropical Cyclones / Infrastructure Resilience
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
Typhoon Danas made landfall on Taiwan's west coast late on July 6, 2025, bringing heavy wind and rain to densely populated areas that are less commonly the direct landfall zone for Taiwan typhoons. Early reporting recorded deaths, hundreds of injuries, widespread closures, transport disruption, flooding, landslides, and major power outages.

### State after 365 days
One year later, Danas stands out in the 2025 Pacific season because of its track and the infrastructure failures it exposed. Later tallies indicated broader casualties and substantial agricultural and private-facility losses, while the storm's west-coast impact remained the notable feature for preparedness planning.

### Direct impacts
The direct impacts included deaths, hundreds of injuries, disrupted ferries and flights, school and office closures, flooding and landslides, and power outages affecting hundreds of thousands of households. The storm also damaged agriculture and private facilities, with losses later reported in the billions of New Taiwan dollars.

### Indirect impacts
Danas reinforced that Taiwan's western plains and agricultural counties cannot treat direct typhoon landfall as an eastern-coast problem only. The power-outage death linked to a failed respirator also showed how storm resilience depends on household medical backup, grid restoration, and local care networks, not only weather warnings.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate coverage captured the wind, rain, and emergency closures, but early figures understated the later injury count, outage persistence, and economic damage. It also took time for the unusual west-coast landfall to become the most analytically important feature.

### One-year assessment
Danas was consequential less for its peak category than for where it struck. It broadened the map of Taiwan's practical typhoon risk and left a record of infrastructure, agriculture, and medical-dependency vulnerabilities that are useful for future preparedness.
