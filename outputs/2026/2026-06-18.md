## 1. The U.S. Supreme Court upholds Tennessee's ban on gender-affirming care for minors

**Original date:** 2025-06-18
**Type:** Civil Rights / Constitutional Law / Health Policy
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-18, the U.S. Supreme Court decided United States v. Skrmetti by a 6-3 vote. The Court upheld Tennessee's restriction on puberty blockers and hormone therapy for minors when used to treat gender dysphoria, holding that the law was not subject to heightened scrutiny under the Equal Protection Clause and survived rational-basis review.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the ruling stands as a central legal marker in the state-by-state fight over transgender health care. It did not resolve every transgender-rights issue, but it gave states a stronger constitutional defense for youth medical-care bans and caused related cases to be reassessed under the Court's narrower equal-protection analysis. Its practical effect was to move many disputes away from a broad sex-discrimination theory and toward state legislation, state constitutional claims, parental-rights claims, and narrower administrative-law challenges.

### Direct impacts
The ruling affirmed the Sixth Circuit judgment and allowed Tennessee's law to remain in effect. It also weakened federal equal-protection challenges to similar state bans by holding that Tennessee's law classified by age and medical use rather than by sex or transgender status.

### Indirect impacts
The decision shifted leverage toward state legislatures and away from federal courts for this category of claims. It also became part of broader litigation over passports, Medicaid coverage, school policy, military service, and sports participation, although those disputes involved different legal questions.

### What original coverage missed
Same-day coverage captured the immediate legal loss for challengers, but it could not yet show how quickly lower courts and federal agencies would try to apply or distinguish Skrmetti in other transgender-rights disputes.

### One-year assessment
Skrmetti was consequential because it set the Supreme Court's first major framework for youth gender-affirming-care bans. The legal meaning was narrower than a complete ruling on all transgender rights, but broad enough to reshape litigation strategy and state policy for the following year.

## 2. The FDA approves twice-yearly lenacapavir for HIV prevention

**Original date:** 2025-06-18
**Type:** Public Health / HIV Prevention / Drug Regulation
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-18, Gilead announced that the U.S. Food and Drug Administration had approved lenacapavir, marketed as Yeztugo, as pre-exposure prophylaxis to reduce the risk of sexually acquired HIV-1 in adults and adolescents weighing at least 35 kilograms. The approval made it the first twice-yearly PrEP option available in the United States.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the approval reads as both a scientific milestone and an access test. Trial data had shown extremely high prevention efficacy, and follow-up developments included European regulatory movement and agreements aimed at much lower prices for many low- and middle-income countries. At the same time, the U.S. list price, insurance uptake, clinic delivery, HIV-testing requirements, and international funding cuts kept access from being automatic.

### Direct impacts
The approval added a six-month injectable prevention option to a field that had relied mainly on daily oral PrEP and shorter-interval injectable PrEP. It offered a way to reduce adherence and stigma barriers for people who could access the drug and the required testing.

### Indirect impacts
Lenacapavir changed the policy conversation around HIV prevention from whether a highly durable tool existed to whether governments, donors, manufacturers, insurers, and clinics could deliver it at scale. It also sharpened the distinction between drug efficacy and public-health impact.

### What original coverage missed
Initial approval coverage could show the clinical promise and early price concerns, but it could not yet show the later global-access negotiations or how much the medicine's impact would depend on procurement, generic timing, and health-system capacity.

### One-year assessment
The FDA approval was consequential because it converted one of the most important recent HIV-prevention discoveries into an authorized product. One year later, the question was no longer only whether lenacapavir worked, but whether its rollout could reach the populations most likely to benefit.

## 3. A leaked Hun Sen call pushes Thailand's governing coalition into crisis

**Original date:** 2025-06-18
**Type:** Government Stability / Coalition Politics / Southeast Asia
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-06-18, audio from a call between Thai Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra and Cambodian Senate President Hun Sen became public amid a border dispute. Paetongtarn confirmed the call's authenticity and defended it as private de-escalation diplomacy, but the Bhumjaithai Party announced it would leave the governing coalition, saying the call had damaged Thailand's dignity and military standing.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the leak was no longer just a scandal over tone or protocol. It had become the starting point for a wider government crisis: Paetongtarn was suspended by Thailand's Constitutional Court on 2025-07-01, removed from office on 2025-08-29, and succeeded after parliamentary maneuvering by Bhumjaithai leader Anutin Charnvirakul.

### Direct impacts
The immediate impact was the loss of a major coalition partner and the weakening of Paetongtarn's parliamentary position. It also deepened nationalist pressure around the Cambodia border dispute and put the prime minister's relationship with the military under scrutiny.

### Indirect impacts
The crisis reopened Thailand's longer pattern of elected governments, courts, military influence, royalist-conservative pressure, and Shinawatra-family vulnerability. It also connected a bilateral border dispute to domestic coalition arithmetic and judicial ethics review.

### What original coverage missed
Initial coverage could identify the coalition rupture, but it could not yet show that the controversy would lead to Paetongtarn's suspension, removal, and replacement by the leader of the party that had exited her coalition.

### One-year assessment
The June 18 leak was consequential because it turned a fragile coalition problem into a constitutional and leadership crisis. Its lasting importance lies in how quickly informal diplomacy, nationalist politics, and court review combined to end another Shinawatra premiership.

## 4. A Nature study estimates large climate-driven losses to staple-crop calories even with adaptation

**Original date:** 2025-06-18
**Type:** Climate Science / Food Security / Agriculture
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
On 2025-06-18, Nature published research by Andrew Hultgren and co-authors estimating the effects of climate change on global agriculture while accounting for adaptation. The study concluded that warming would still reduce production of key crops, with losses measured in calories per person per day for each additional degree of warming.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the paper's value is as a reference point in the adaptation debate. It did not forecast immediate famine, and it carried the usual uncertainty of empirical crop modeling. Its lasting contribution was to challenge the assumption that farmers' adaptation and income growth would neutralize warming's effect on staple-crop output.

### Direct impacts
The study provided a quantified estimate for how warming could reduce caloric production even after farmers adjust practices and crop choices. It focused attention on breadbasket regions and on the limits of adaptation as a substitute for emissions reduction.

### Indirect impacts
The findings strengthened arguments for treating agricultural adaptation, crop research, trade resilience, and food-security planning as climate-policy priorities. They also gave policymakers and analysts a sharper metric than yield percentages alone: daily calories at risk per person.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate coverage could summarize the headline estimate, but it could not show how the study would be absorbed into later policy debates or how competing crop models would revise or challenge its assumptions.

### One-year assessment
The publication was consequential as a research event rather than a policy decision. One year later, its significance was the disciplined warning that adaptation helps but does not erase climate damage to staple agriculture.
