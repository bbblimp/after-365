## 1. Moody's removes the United States' last major triple-A sovereign rating

**Original date:** 2025-05-16
**Type:** Public Finance / Sovereign Credit
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-16, Moody's Ratings downgraded the United States' long-term issuer and senior unsecured ratings from Aaa to Aa1 and changed the outlook to stable. The agency cited rising federal debt, widening deficits, and interest costs that had become high relative to similarly rated sovereigns.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the downgrade had not produced a discrete funding crisis, but it remained a marker of a larger fiscal trend. In its 2026 budget outlook, the Congressional Budget Office projected federal debt held by the public rising from about the size of annual GDP to a record 120 percent of GDP by 2036, with rising net interest costs a central driver.

### Direct impacts
The direct effect was reputational and market-facing: the United States no longer carried a top sovereign rating from any of the three major rating agencies. The action also gave fiscal-risk arguments a concrete benchmark during budget negotiations.

### Indirect impacts
The downgrade reinforced concern that high debt and interest costs were becoming a structural constraint on U.S. policymaking. It also showed that the dollar's reserve-currency role and the depth of Treasury markets could coexist with deteriorating rating-agency views of federal fiscal governance.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate coverage could see the symbolic importance, but it could not yet know whether Congress would materially alter the fiscal path. One year later, official projections still pointed toward rising debt and interest burdens rather than a resolved problem.

### One-year assessment
The downgrade was consequential less because it immediately changed Treasury market functioning than because it closed a long reputational chapter. After 365 days, it reads as a formal acknowledgement that U.S. fiscal strength and U.S. fiscal governance were no longer being evaluated as the same thing.

## 2. France files an ICJ case against Iran over detained nationals and consular protection

**Original date:** 2025-05-16
**Type:** International Law / Consular Protection
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-16, France instituted proceedings against Iran at the International Court of Justice. The application alleged serious and repeated breaches of the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations in connection with the arrest, detention, and trial of French nationals in Iran.

### State after 365 days
Within the following year, the case's central human stakes changed. Cécile Kohler and Jacques Paris, the French nationals most associated with the dispute, were allowed to leave Iran and returned to France in April 2026 after more than three years of detention and a period of restricted movement at the French embassy in Tehran.

### Direct impacts
The filing moved the dispute from diplomatic protest into formal litigation at the UN's principal judicial organ. It created a public legal record around consular access, detention conditions, and France's claim that Iran was using detained nationals as leverage.

### Indirect impacts
The case showed how states may use international litigation to structure pressure in hostage-diplomacy disputes, even when release ultimately depends on negotiation, mediation, or exchange arrangements outside the courtroom.

### What original coverage missed
Original coverage could not know that the two detainees would return to France before the one-year mark. It also could not yet show whether the ICJ filing would be a decisive legal lever or one part of a broader diplomatic process.

### One-year assessment
The ICJ filing mattered because it converted a consular and human-rights dispute into a formal state responsibility claim. The later return of Kohler and Paris gave the episode a concrete humanitarian outcome, but the path to that outcome remained diplomatic as much as judicial.

## 3. FDA grants full approval to Novavax's COVID-19 vaccine, but with narrower use

**Original date:** 2025-05-16
**Type:** Health Regulation / Vaccines
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-16, the FDA approved Nuvaxovid, Novavax's protein-based COVID-19 vaccine, but limited the indication to people 65 and older and to people ages 12 through 64 with at least one condition putting them at high risk for severe COVID-19.

### State after 365 days
After 365 days, the approval stood as a transition point in U.S. COVID vaccine regulation. Novavax gained full licensure for the country's non-mRNA COVID vaccine option, while later FDA and CDC actions moved COVID vaccination policy further away from broad emergency-era availability and toward risk-based recommendations.

### Direct impacts
The decision replaced emergency authorization with full approval for Novavax's vaccine, preserved a protein-based alternative to mRNA vaccines, and triggered commercial planning with Sanofi for the 2025-2026 formula.

### Indirect impacts
The restricted label signaled a more cautious regulatory posture toward COVID boosters for lower-risk people. It also put insurers, pharmacies, clinicians, and patients in a more complicated position over eligibility and access.

### What original coverage missed
Immediate coverage could identify the unusual limitation, but it could not yet show how the approval would fit into the wider 2025-2026 seasonal vaccine process or how much practical access would depend on follow-on recommendations and distribution.

### One-year assessment
The approval was consequential because it both expanded and narrowed vaccine policy at the same time: it gave Novavax a durable license, but under conditions that previewed a more targeted federal approach to COVID vaccination.

## 4. JWST result pushes the confirmed galaxy frontier to MoM-z14

**Original date:** 2025-05-16
**Type:** Science / Astronomy
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
On 2025-05-16, researchers posted a paper reporting MoM-z14, a luminous galaxy in the COSMOS field with spectroscopic redshift z = 14.44. The result placed the galaxy's observed light at roughly 280 million years after the Big Bang and pushed the confirmed observational frontier closer to cosmic dawn.

### State after 365 days
By early 2026, NASA and ESA public materials described MoM-z14 as the farthest galaxy detected and spectroscopically confirmed by JWST, while also noting that record status in this field may be temporary. The result remained scientifically useful because it added another unexpectedly luminous early galaxy to the growing JWST sample.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was scientific rather than operational: the paper extended the spectroscopically confirmed high-redshift galaxy record and added data for testing models of early star formation, galaxy growth, and reionization.

### Indirect impacts
MoM-z14 strengthened a broader JWST pattern in which early galaxies appear brighter, more chemically evolved, or more numerous than many pre-JWST expectations. That pattern has forced theorists to refine rather than simply discard models of early galaxy formation.

### What original coverage missed
The initial preprint established the claim, but later agency releases and publication context made clearer how the object fit into the accumulating JWST picture of cosmic dawn.

### One-year assessment
MoM-z14's importance is not only that it held a distance record. Its value after 365 days is that it became one more well-characterized data point showing that the early universe was observationally richer than earlier instruments could demonstrate.
