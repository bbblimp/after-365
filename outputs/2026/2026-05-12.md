# After 365 - 2026-05-12

## Daily Summary

For the 2025-05-12 lookback date, the selected items center on developments whose importance became clearer through follow-up: the U.S. and China stepped back from prohibitive tariffs but left structural tensions unresolved; the PKK announced dissolution after four decades of conflict while political questions remained open; Gaza's famine warning later became a confirmed famine and then a still-fragile food emergency; and a U.S. drug-pricing order moved from broad executive direction into voluntary manufacturer agreements and implementation claims.

## 1. United States and China announce a 90-day tariff de-escalation in Geneva

**Original date:** 2025-05-12
**Type:** Economy / Trade
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-12, the United States and China issued a Geneva joint statement after weekend talks, agreeing to suspend or remove parts of the tariff escalation that had pushed duties on each other's goods to prohibitive levels. The United States said it would reduce the recently imposed tariff burden on Chinese goods, while China agreed to lower its retaliatory tariffs for the same 90-day period.

### State after 365 days
The Geneva pause did not resolve the underlying trade dispute, but it became the template for further managed extensions. A later White House statement in August 2025 explicitly recalled the Geneva framework and continued the suspension for another 90 days, keeping the dispute inside a negotiating process rather than a full tariff rupture.

### Direct impacts
The immediate impact was a reduction in tariff pressure and a market rally after fears of a near trade embargo between the world's two largest economies. It also created a formal consultation mechanism for further talks.

### Indirect impacts
The episode showed both the leverage and limits of tariff escalation. Businesses gained temporary relief, but planning remained difficult because the truce was time-limited and tariffs stayed higher than before the April escalation.

### What original coverage missed
Initial coverage could register the market relief, but it could not show whether Geneva was a settlement or a pause. Follow-up showed it was mainly a stabilizing mechanism that postponed sharper confrontation while leaving structural disputes unresolved.

### One-year assessment
The Geneva statement mattered because it prevented an immediate breakdown in U.S.-China goods trade and set a pattern of extensions. Its consequence was not a durable trade settlement, but a shift from uncontrolled escalation to rolling, conditional management of the dispute.

## 2. PKK announces it will disband and end its armed struggle

**Original date:** 2025-05-12
**Type:** Conflict / Politics
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-12, the Kurdistan Workers' Party said it would dissolve its organizational structure and end its armed struggle, following a call from imprisoned founder Abdullah Ocalan. The announcement pointed to a possible close to a conflict with the Turkish state that began in 1984 and killed more than 40,000 people.

### State after 365 days
The announcement was followed by symbolic and practical steps, including reported weapon-burning in July and an October 2025 statement that PKK militants would withdraw from Turkey as part of a disarmament process. The process still depended on political arrangements, legal treatment of fighters, and broader Kurdish-rights questions that were not settled by the May declaration alone.

### Direct impacts
The direct impact was a formal end-of-armed-struggle declaration by a group long designated as terrorist by Turkey, the United States, and the European Union. Turkish officials and international actors treated it as a potential opening for a new peace process.

### Indirect impacts
The announcement affected regional calculations beyond Turkey, including northern Iraq and Syria, where Kurdish armed groups and political movements remain connected to wider security arrangements.

### What original coverage missed
The first reports could identify the historical scale of the decision, but they could not determine whether disarmament would be operational, whether hardline factions would comply, or whether Turkey would pair the move with political reforms.

### One-year assessment
The PKK declaration was consequential as a formal break with decades of insurgency. One year later, its importance lies in opening a real but incomplete transition from armed conflict toward political negotiation, with the hardest institutional questions still unresolved.

## 3. IPC-linked agencies warn Gaza is at critical risk of famine

**Original date:** 2025-05-12
**Type:** Humanitarian / War
**Confidence:** High

### Then
On 2025-05-12, the World Food Programme and UNICEF cited a new IPC snapshot warning that Gaza's entire population faced acute food insecurity and that 470,000 people were in catastrophic hunger conditions. The warning followed renewed fighting and the blocking of aid into Gaza from early March.

### State after 365 days
The warning was borne out in later IPC-linked findings. In August 2025, UN agencies said famine had been confirmed in Gaza Governorate, with more than half a million people trapped in famine conditions. By December 2025, OCHA reported that famine conditions had been offset after the October ceasefire improved food access, but the situation remained critical, with large numbers still facing severe acute food insecurity.

### Direct impacts
The May warning gave humanitarian agencies a quantified, internationally recognized basis for urgent calls to restore access, scale up aid, and prevent famine-related deaths.

### Indirect impacts
The finding became part of the evidentiary record around civilian harm, aid restrictions, and the conduct of the war. It also made later assessments measurable against a clear May baseline.

### What original coverage missed
The May reports warned of a critical risk, but they could not yet establish the later formal famine classification or the limited improvement after a later ceasefire. The one-year record shows both deterioration and fragility after partial relief.

### One-year assessment
The May 12 warning was consequential because it was not a speculative alarm. It accurately marked a worsening path that led to a formal famine finding, and later improvement did not erase the scale of the humanitarian failure it documented.

## 4. Trump signs most-favored-nation drug-pricing executive order

**Original date:** 2025-05-12
**Type:** Health Policy
**Confidence:** Medium

### Then
On 2025-05-12, President Donald Trump signed an executive order directing his administration to pursue most-favored-nation prescription drug pricing, aiming to align U.S. prices for some drugs with lower prices paid in comparable countries. The order directed federal agencies to communicate price targets, pursue a direct-purchase mechanism, and use trade tools against foreign pricing practices the administration considered unfair.

### State after 365 days
Follow-up showed the order was not self-executing. HHS and CMS announced price targets on May 20, and the White House later said it had reached voluntary agreements with major manufacturers beginning in September 2025. By May 2026, the administration described a broader MFN framework and reported voluntary agreements with 17 large manufacturers, while also seeking legislation to codify the approach.

### Direct impacts
The order gave federal agencies a formal mandate to pressure manufacturers and develop mechanisms for lower prices, but immediate consumer price reductions depended on later implementation rather than the signature itself.

### Indirect impacts
The policy shifted drug-price debate toward international reference pricing, manufacturer agreements, and the relationship between U.S. prices and foreign health systems. It also increased pressure on pharmaceutical companies in negotiations with the administration.

### What original coverage missed
Initial coverage captured the ambition and uncertainty. Follow-up clarified that the practical path ran through agency targets, voluntary deals, direct-purchase infrastructure, and possible legislation rather than automatic nationwide price cuts.

### One-year assessment
The executive order was consequential as a policy trigger rather than an immediate price event. Its durable significance depends on whether voluntary agreements and any later statutory framework produce measurable, sustained savings for patients and public programs.
